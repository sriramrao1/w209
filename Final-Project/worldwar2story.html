Olympics as a proxy for World Order

Olympic Events leading up to World War II

World War II is considered to have officially started on Sept 1, 1939 with the german invasion of Poland. However, the effects of Nazi Fascism started with Hitler becoming the German Chancellor on Jan 30, 1933. In April 1933, Germany instituted a "Aryans-only" policy in all athletic organozations. Jewish athletes were barred from German sports clubs.

  • 1936 Olympics - Berlin, Germany

    In April 1933, an "Aryans only" policy was instituted in all German athletic organizations.For the first time in the history of the modern Olympic Games, United States and Europe called for a boycott of the Olympics because of the host country’s abuse of human rights. As a token gesture to placate international opinion, German authorities allowed the star fencer Helene Mayer to represent Germany at the Olympic Games in Berlin.

    The Nazi regime tried to camouflage its violent racist policies while it hosted the event. Most anti-Jewish signs were temporarily removed and newspapers toned down their harsh rhetoric to present foreign spectators and journalists with a false image of a peaceful, tolerant Germany.

    Germany emerged victorious from the XIth Olympiad. German athletes captured the most medals, and German hospitality and organization won the praises of visitors. Some even found reason to hope that this peaceable interlude would endure. Only a few reporters, understood that the Berlin glitter was merely a facade hiding a racist and oppressively violent regime.

  • 1940 Olympics - Tokyo, Japan

    Officially known as the Games of the XII Olympiad and originally scheduled to be held from 21 September to 6 October 1940, in Tokyo, Japan, were cancelled due to the outbreak of World War II. The Olympic Games were suspended indefinitely following the outbreak of World War II and did not resume until the London Games of 1948.

  • 1948 Olympics, London, Great Britain

    Olympics resumed after the World War with Germany and Japan not participating due to the damages to their country’s infrastructure and economy from the World War.
